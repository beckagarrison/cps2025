## üìö KEY CASE LAW DATABASE

### **Fourth Amendment Cases:**

#### **Gates v. Texas Dept. of Protective Services**
- **Citation:** 537 F.3d 404 (5th Cir. 2008)
- **Holding:** CPS needs probable cause + warrant/consent/exigency
- **Key Quote:** "Right of parents to raise children without undue government intrusion is fundamental liberty interest"
- **Application:** Use when CPS entered home without warrant

#### **Doe v. Heck**
- **Citation:** 327 F.3d 492 (7th Cir. 2003)
- **Holding:** Strip search without warrant violates Fourth Amendment
- **Key Quote:** "Absent emergency, strip search requires warrant or court order"
- **Application:** Use for invasive medical examinations

#### **Roska ex rel. Roska v. Sneddon**
- **Citation:** 437 F.3d 964 (9th Cir. 2006)
- **Holding:** School interrogation requires reasonable suspicion
- **Key Quote:** "Social workers must have objectively reasonable suspicion before investigation"
- **Application:** Child interviewed without reasonable suspicion

---

### **Due Process Cases:**

#### **Santosky v. Kramer**
- **Citation:** 455 U.S. 745 (1982)
- **Holding:** Clear and convincing evidence required
- **Key Quote:** "Fundamental liberty interest requires clear and convincing standard"
- **Application:** Foundation for burden of proof

#### **Troxel v. Granville**
- **Citation:** 530 U.S. 57 (2000)
- **Holding:** Parents have fundamental right to make decisions
- **Key Quote:** "Perhaps the oldest of the fundamental liberty interests"
- **Application:** Establish fundamental nature of parental rights

#### **Stanley v. Illinois**
- **Citation:** 405 U.S. 645 (1972)
- **Holding:** Hearing required before children taken
- **Key Quote:** "Procedural due process rules shaped by risk of error"
- **Application:** Parent denied hearing or procedural protections

---

### **Qualified Immunity Cases:**

#### **Pearson v. Callahan**
- **Citation:** 555 U.S. 223 (2009)
- **Holding:** Two-part test for qualified immunity
- **Application:** Overcome qualified immunity defense

#### **Tenenbaum v. Williams**
- **Citation:** 193 F.3d 581 (2d Cir. 1999)
- **Holding:** No immunity for unconstitutional removal
- **Key Quote:** "Qualified immunity does not protect clearly unconstitutional removal"
- **Application:** Defeats immunity when removal clearly unconstitutional
