### **2. First Amendment Religious Freedom in CPS Cases**

**Category:** Bible & Religious Freedom  
**Subcategory:** Constitutional Rights  
**Word Count:** ~2,200 words

#### **Content Includes:**

**First Amendment Religion Clauses:**
- Establishment Clause - Government cannot establish religion
- Free Exercise Clause - Government cannot prohibit religious practice

**What CPS CANNOT Do:**
1. âŒ Target families based on religion
2. âŒ Interfere with religious upbringing
3. âŒ Impose secular values
4. âŒ Remove children for "too religious"
5. âŒ Prohibit religious instruction
6. âŒ Force public school attendance
7. âŒ Restrict religious activities as condition of reunification

**Key Supreme Court Cases:**

**Pierce v. Society of Sisters (1925)**
- Parents have right to direct education including religious
- "Child is not the mere creature of the State"
- Applied to CPS: Cannot force secular education

**Wisconsin v. Yoder (1972)**
- Amish parents' right to religious upbringing
- Free Exercise protects parental religious decisions
- Test: Compelling state interest + least restrictive means
- Applied to CPS: Cannot remove unless actual harm + no alternative

**Employment Division v. Smith (1990)**
- Narrowed religious exemptions BUT
- Parental rights + religious freedom = heightened protection
- *Yoder* remains good law

**Church of Lukumi Babalu Aye v. Hialeah (1993)**
- Laws targeting specific religions violate Free Exercise
- Applied to CPS: Targeting religious families = strict scrutiny

**Fulton v. City of Philadelphia (2021)**
- Cannot discriminate against religious foster/adoptive parents
- Religious agencies protected

**Religious Freedom Restoration Act (RFRA):**
- Federal RFRA + many state RFRAs
- Government cannot substantially burden religion unless:
  1. Compelling interest
  2. Least restrictive means

**Common CPS Religious Freedom Issues:**

**1. Homeschooling**
- Protected by *Pierce* + *Yoder*
- Cannot remove for religious homeschooling
- Cannot require public school

**2. Medical Decisions**
- Faith healing, prayer, refusing treatments
- Balance: Non-emergency = parents' choice; Life-threatening = state may intervene
- Must show actual danger, not speculation

**3. Discipline**
- Biblical discipline vs. abuse distinction
- Religious belief in discipline protected
- Does not permit abuse (injury/excessive force)

**4. Family Structure**
- Traditional gender roles protected
- Large families not neglect
- Cannot force feminist/secular values

**5. Foster/Adoption**
- *Fulton* protects religious agencies
- Cannot force placement violating beliefs

**Defense Strategy:**
1. Assert First Amendment rights
2. Invoke RFRA
3. Cite *Yoder* and *Pierce*
4. Demand compelling interest
5. Argue least restrictive means
6. Challenge selective enforcement
7. Distinguish belief from harm

**Accommodations Required:**
- Visitation not conflicting with religious observances
- Religious instruction for child in foster care
- Placement with same faith when possible
- Religious services participation
- Dietary restrictions respected
