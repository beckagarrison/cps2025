### **2. Maxims of Law (3)**

Ancient legal principles still applicable today:

#### **A. Ignorantia juris non excusat**
- **Translation:** "Ignorance of the law excuses no one"
- **Application:** CPS workers must know and follow the law
- **Defense Use:** Challenge "good faith" claims when rights violated

#### **B. Ei incumbit probatio qui dicit, non qui negat**
- **Translation:** "The burden of proof lies upon him who affirms, not him who denies"
- **Application:** State must prove allegations, not parent disprove them
- **Defense Use:** Object when burden improperly shifted to parent

#### **C. Nemo tenetur seipsum accusare**
- **Translation:** "No one is bound to accuse himself"
- **Application:** Fifth Amendment privilege applies in CPS cases
- **Defense Use:** Assert right to remain silent without negative inference

### **3. Federalist Papers (2)**

Founding Father wisdom on government power and limits:

#### **A. Federalist No. 51 - Separation of Powers**
- **Author:** James Madison
- **Topic:** Why government power must be limited and checked
- **Key Quote:** "If men were angels, no government would be necessary..."
- **Application:** 
  - CPS cannot be investigator, prosecutor, AND judge
  - Separation of powers prevents tyranny
  - Checks and balances required
- **Defense Use:** Challenge CPS overreach and concentration of power

#### **B. Federalist No. 78 - Judicial Review**
- **Author:** Alexander Hamilton
- **Topic:** Courts as guardians of constitutional rights
- **Key Quote:** "Courts must declare all acts contrary to the Constitution void"
- **Application:**
  - Courts must check executive (CPS) overreach
  - Judicial independence protects rights
  - Courts are "bulwark against tyranny"
- **Defense Use:** Remind courts of duty to enforce constitutional limits

The supreme law of the land:

- **Full Text:** Protection against unreasonable searches and seizures
- **Application to CPS:**
  - Home entry requires warrant or valid exception
  - Consent must be voluntary
  - Exigent circumstances must be genuine emergency
  - Child removal = seizure under Fourth Amendment
- **Common Violations:**
  - Warrantless home entry
  - Coerced consent
  - Searches beyond scope
  - Removals without probable cause

#### **B. Fourteenth Amendment Due Process & Equal Protection**
- **Full Text:** Due process and equal protection clauses
- **Application to CPS:**
  - Parental rights are fundamental liberty interest
  - State needs compelling interest to interfere
  - Procedural safeguards required
  - Notice, hearing, counsel rights
- **Common Violations:**
  - Insufficient notice
  - Denial of hearing rights
  - Removal without due process
  - Discrimination

### **5. Magna Carta (1)**

800-year foundation of due process:

#### **Clause 39: Foundation of Due Process**
- **Original Latin:** "Nullus liber homo capiatur..."
- **Translation:** "No free man shall be seized or imprisoned...except by lawful judgment"
- **Historical Significance:**
  - Direct ancestor of Fifth/Fourteenth Amendments
  - Foundation of rule of law
  - Limits on government power
- **Application:** 
  - CPS cannot seize children without due process
  - Requires judicial review, not just CPS decision
  - 800-year tradition violated by warrantless removals
- **Defense Use:** Show deep historical roots of rights violated

### **7. Legal Doctrines (1)**

Understanding state authority and its limits:

#### **Parens Patriae: The State's Limited Authority**
- **Definition:** State as "parent of the nation"
- **Historical Origin:** English common law
- **Critical Limitation:** Does NOT override constitutional rights
- **Two-Part Test:**
  1. Child actually needs protection
  2. Parents unable or unwilling to protect
- **Common Misuse:**
  - CPS claims unlimited authority
  - Overrides Fourth Amendment
  - Bypasses due process
  - Presumes state knows best
- **Proper Limits:**
  - Subject to constitutional constraints
  - Requires proof of harm
  - Narrowly tailored
  - Least restrictive alternative
- **Defense Use:** Challenge overreach, demand constitutional compliance
